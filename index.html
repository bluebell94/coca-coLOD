About The Project

This Project Coca-CoLOD, belongs to the 'Knowledge Representation and Extraction' course of University of Bologna, a.y. 2018/2019.

The purpose of the project is to extract information and represent knowledge of literary and structuralist critism introduced in Liguistics by Saussaure on a few examples, part of the advertising campaign of Coca-Cola “Taste the feeling”, in LOD environment.

Case Study Scenario

Aims and Goals of the Project

This project aims to analyse the video advertisement as whole and also in its sub-parts – the song, the scenes of the video and the video advertisement as a whole as well as the slogans shown during the ad. Apart from that a goal of the project is also to represent the messages which are not explicit and remain non-verbal but are transmitted through the advertising video. All this will be done by applying certain computational tools. Based on the computational results will be created a knowledge graph which illustrates the structure behind the advertising strategy of Coca-Cola campaign “taste the feeling”, what kind of approaches they are using in order to tackle the target audience and will make explicit the connection between all this and the structuralism of Saussaure(signified, signifier).

Concept of strategy

The managers of Coca-Cola are trying to tackle the young people, especially those who are still not fans of the product and encourage them to try it. The Coca-Cola product is presented within different aspects of our daily life and associated with a wide variety of actions, feelings and emotional states. So as to be able to apply it in any kind of event and situation. The idea is to associate the product with any kind of action that the public might prefer and any situation from our daily life. What is in the core is to promote the concept of having this carbonated drink as a companion in our everyday life.

Communication model

In the advertisement of Coca-Cola we are able to observe 2 ways of communication – verbal and non-verbal. This is a strategic move that brings a lot to this campaign as the communication tries to convey a particular message, to encourage customers’interest and to popularize a certain brand. By using both verbal and non-verbal communication, what the target audience receives also much complex messages from the non-verbal communication methods. The non-verbal communication goes far beyond what is on surface and what is obvious and well-known. It is trying to construct a concept, by employing the advantages of verbal and non-verbal communication so that the message that is being transmitted to the customer is stronger and the approach is on different levels, not just by text messages, but also by visual representations.

Onthology Development

By taking into consideration the resuls that have been reached by applying a variety of tools, the most essential and the basic structure is been extracted the knowledge that comes out of this case-study, so that it is able to be reused in similar case-scenarios. Coca-CoLOD will include basic information about the advertising campaign, its strategical concept,described by the authors, its language usage in the slogans, distinction between verbal and non-verbal communication and their application and structuralist analysis in the domain of the semiotics, trying to point out the layers from which consists this successful advertisement.The classes that are created in Coca-CoLOD by using Protégé, the ontology development environment for the Web, is shown via WebVowl.

Semiotics in Advertising

Semiotics are frequently used in advertising as an approach to transmit an advertiser's message through the usage of signs or symbols. A sign by its own can be better understood in different ways as a signifier – having more or less literal meaning, or as a symbol that signifies points to else. In some cases, the sign can be an exact representation of the thing being signified, while in other cases, it may be a symbol associated with it. The use of semiotics in advertising is in many ways much more connected with the process than with a single action, because by the tools of the semiotics the advertisers are able to create not just positive feeling, but also loyalty towards a brand. This process of branding trequires much longer time and is being done by employing repetition, combinations of symbols, bringing words, images and music together to create one whole completed concept, that the customer will associate with the single product and consequently with the brand.

So that the supposed activity or action is left to be imagined by the public. The verbal approach is also observed in the lyrics of the song of the advertising video. Semiotics in Advertising Part 1

Linguistic-Semiotic Analysis

Repetitions

The majority of the words as we see are used twice in the insciptions, and we are seeing an obvious repetition. One phrase finishes with a certain word and the consequent ones starts with the same word. This further makes us more concentrated on the meaning and leaves us time to employ our imagination, creativity and past experiences in order to create our own concept strating from a few keywords. Most of the words imply the presence of more people like for example – “love”, “secrets”, “fun”, “friends”, “stories”, “strangers”. In all of these cases we have the implication that there is more than one person. That what is being experienced is being shared. This is like a continuation of the effect of the preposition “with”, implies that there is not just some connection between the nouns, but that in most of the cases we talk about a group of at least 2 people.

Semiotics in Advertising Part 1

Liguistic-Semiotic Analysis

Metaphors

Conceptual Metaphors

In the food advertising the use of conceptual metaphors is quite extensive in order to arouse customers’ interest. By conceptualization of different aspects related to the product into various entites and objects, the marketing strategists present us one whole conceptual idea, not just a single product. The idea is to provide us with many different scenarious of its usage and application, to show how us all of its advantages and how it could change our lifestyle. In the advertisement of Coca-Cola “Taste the feeling”, we are able to observe this in the following slogans :” Fire with Coca-Cola”, “Coca-Cola with smiles”, "Fun with Coca-Cola". These slogans can’t be interpreted literally, but provide us the idea of an association with gathering around fire with friends,laughing and sharing something funny in a cosy atmosphere or about sentiments of affection and the beginning of a relationship. These phrases create the idea of Coca-Cola as a product that makes you feel positive, relaxed and in a cosy and friendly atmosphere.

Onthological Metaphors

Ontological metaphors are used often in order to be interpreted as entity and are based on our past experience. In our case in the phrase “Taste the feeling”, we are able to interpret “feeling” as an entity, because it refers to a sequence of events happeningthat have the task to create this so called “feeling. The feeling itself involves different sensations and emotional states. In some people tasting Coca-Cola, might provoke hunger, a crave to drink even more of it, happiness, satisfaction, stimulationby others – even anxiety if they are not used to high caffeine and blood-sugar levels. The feeling that is trying to be communicated by Coca-Cola marketeers is much more like whole experience with many stages, because the idea behind the main slogan “Taste the feeling” has the implication that it last for longer time, it is not just a single moment in time, but a feeling that is being followed by a sequence of positive emotions.

Personification Metaphors

Metaphors connected with personification are also used in order to create the overall idea of Coca-Cola.We are able to observe such in the following inscriptions: “Coca-Cola with smiles”, “Coca-Cola with love”, “Fun with Coca-Cola’’, “ You with Coca-Cola”. In all of the following phrases when we are talking about Coca-Cola, no matter that we know that it is a non-alcoholic beverage, our mind makes an association with somebody or a group of people, because usually we are smiling when we are around friends, when we talk about love we always think about more than one person. When we are trying to imagine having fun, it is always associated with a group of friends, relatives, family, etc. Because all of this, the image of Cocca-Cola becomes personificated and the idea about has and added value to the literal meaning as a carbonated refreshing drink in a glass bottle.

Semiotics in Advertising Part 2

Saussaure Structuralism

In this advertising campaign we are able to see the structuralism employed by Saussaure. By definition structuralism is a system in which each element in a group can only be understood by its relation ti other elements as part of a larger structure.in its core is the study about conveying messages. His theory is based on the idea that each language is made up pf signs. Furthermore, each sign is made up pf two parts – a signifier and a signified. The signifier is the pointing finger, the word, the sound-image. It is in the interpretation of the signifier that meaning is created.

The signified is the concept, the meaning, the thing indicated by the signifier. It doesn't need to be a 'real object' but is some referent to which the signifier refers. The thing signified is created in the perceiver and is internal to them. Whilst we share concepts, we do so via signifiers. Whilst the signifier is more stable, the signified varies between people and contexts. The signified does stabilize with habit, as the signifier cues thoughts and images. In the example of the advertising strategy applied by Coca-Cola in this video – we are able to see how the authors of the ad are trying to focus our attention on the signified. They are allowing us the free our mind, be creative and employ our imagination and so that to constitute whatever relationship we would like with their product. For some Coca-Cola signifies refreshing taste, for others boost of energy, for some it reminds of eating pizza with friends and many other interpretations that are more or less connected with personal stories and experiences.
